PHASE NOTES

Base · W1–8 (Jun–Jul)

Build the durability bank while running is restricted.

  • Job: grow the aerobic engine (bike/row), bank vert by hiking, and condition the quads with downhill running — all green-lit for the current status.
  • Strength: heaviest of the whole plan. Rotate T1 → T2 → T3; deloads use T4. This is where strength has the most value, so don't skip it.
  • Venues: ease in locally (Tegg's, Lyme), build toward Edale/Bowden for the longer days. First B2B in W7.
  • Gate: W8 Effer check decides whether running reintroduction starts in W9.

Build · W9–16 (Aug–Sep)

Turn the base into race-specific mountain fitness.

  • Job: reintroduce running (if cleared), blend run/hike, ramp vert and stack back-to-back weekends. Night practice and course recon begin.
  • Strength: maintain, don't build — rotate T1–T3, but drop to T4 on the big B2B/recon weeks (W14–15) so it doesn't add fatigue.
  • Venues: Edale becomes the keystone; Mam Tor for runnable reintro descents; Costa Blanca recon (W14–15) for the long sustained descents the Peak can't give.

Peak · W17–19 (Oct)

The biggest, most race-like work — then start shedding fatigue.

  • Job: peak vert (W17, ~4,000 m) and peak time on feet (W18, the full dress rehearsal). W18 rehearses everything — kit, nutrition, night, pacing.
  • Strength: maintenance only (T4 → T5). Protect the mountain days; strength must not blunt them.
  • Venues: Edale night B2Bs in the multi-loop car-aided format; a final long rehearsal (Edale or Costa Blanca).

Taper · W20–22 + Race · W23 (Oct–Nov)

Arrive in Finestrat fresh, springy and uninjured.

  • Job: cut volume progressively, keep a little sharpness, sleep and eat well. Resist the urge to "test" fitness — the work is banked.
  • Strength: T5 maintenance only, fading to activation. Last real session ~7–10 days out.
  • Venues: local and low-fuss. W23 is travel + a short shakeout; plan a Friday-afternoon nap before the 23:00 start.

COURSE-SPECIFIC REMINDERS

  • Puig Campana climb by ~km5, in the dark, on fresh legs — the discipline test. Go out far too easy.
  • Aitana (~1,558 m, the roof) around km70 — caps a long high section; this is the crux of the day.
  • Late saw-tooth (Sella ~km80, climbs again to ~km95) — brutal on tired legs. The peak B2Bs rehearse exactly this: climbing when already wrecked.
  • Night-fresh running — the unusual demand of a 23:00 start. Practise it specifically from W13, plus the Friday-afternoon nap.

THE REHAB LINE RUNS THROUGH EVERYTHING

The whole plan is gated on the left kinetic chain staying healthy. Descent reps and the run reintroduction are the highest-risk elements — keep them inside what Effer signs off, watch how things settle over 24–48h, and don't let a target number on this sheet override what the body is telling you. A finish line in November beats a hero week in July.
