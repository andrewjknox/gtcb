THE EVENT

The GTCB 102K is the flagship of Lurbel Costa Blanca Trails (CBT), organised by Club de Montaña Reto 8000 of Elche with the town hall of Finestrat, over 13–15 November 2026. The route crosses the Aitana, Puig Campana and La Serrella sierras, climbing their main summits, with start and finish in Plaza Unión Europea, Finestrat.

  • 101.7 km, one single stage, run in semi-self-sufficiency (see below). Starts Friday 13 November at 23:00 — the first ~7 hours are in the dark.
  • 24-hour limit: the finish closes Saturday at 23:00.
  • Minimum age 21 on race day.
  • Finish inside the limit and you get the finisher medal, a downloadable diploma, and a time in the general and category classifications.

COURSE RULES

  • The route is marked with brightly coloured markers and flags at intervals that vary with the terrain — finding and following the markings is your responsibility. Following the marked itinerary is mandatory.
  • Roads and village streets are not closed to traffic. Normal traffic rules apply; carriageways must be crossed at the signed crossing points.
  • Expect mountain terrain, night running, and possible cold, rain and wind — clothing, shoes and fitness must match the undertaking.
  • Carry your rubbish to the finish or the marked points. Dropping litter anywhere else is disqualification.
  • Poles are allowed, but abandoning them or handing them to anyone anywhere on the course is penalised. A broken pole may be left at an aid station.

SELF-SUFFICIENCY & AID

The race runs in semi-self-sufficiency: between aid stations you organise your own food, clothing and safety, and are expected to solve foreseeable mountain problems without help.

  • At the start you must carry a minimum of 1 litre of water and 500 kcal of food.
  • Aid stations stock food and drink in reasonable quantities, to be consumed on the spot. No cups are provided — you carry your own container.
  • Outside assistance only at the five Crew points (Helipuerto Polop, Benimantell, Confrides, Font de Partagat, Sella), inside the zones reserved for it. Receiving help anywhere else is penalised, accident and emergency excepted.

DROPPING OUT

  • If you abandon, do it at an aid station.
  • The Evac points (Helipuerto Polop, Font del Pi, Benimantell, Confrides, Font de Partagat, Sella) are the only places where a vehicle back to the finish is guaranteed. At other controls you'll be directed onward or wait for the control to close.
  • Injury is the exception — that triggers the emergency response wherever you are.

If weather or force majeure stops the race, entry fees are not refunded; an interrupted race is classified on order and time at the interruption point, or failing that the last control passed.

DROP BAG — CONFRIDES, 58.5 km

Bag
  • A bag is issued with your bib. Fill it with whatever you might need at Confrides (58.5 km, closes 13:30), fit the organisation's label, seal it with nothing sticking out, and hand it in before the start.
  • At Confrides, take what you need and put back what you're done with; it's resealed and returned to Finestrat when that aid station closes — it may well arrive after you do.
  • Collection: Saturday until the finish closes, or Sunday 10:00–14:00. Uncollected bags go to Elche (collectable to 30 November); courier delivery costs €20.

CATEGORIES & TROPHIES

Individual: Absolute (everyone), plus sub-categories — Senior 21–39, Veteran 40–49, Master A 50–59, Master B 60+.

Pairs: mixed, female or male. Both members must pass every control and finish together, no more than 30 seconds apart at the line. Pair runners are classified individually too — if one abandons, the other keeps their individual result. Prizes are cumulative.

Trophies go to the first three in every category. Skip the ceremony and the trophy is deemed renounced.

PENALTIES & DISQUALIFICATION

Minor 3 MINUTES – ½ HOUR

  • Bib not visible on your front, or modified in form or composition.

Serious ½ HOUR – 2 HOURS

  • Jumping the start signal.
  • Missing any piece of mandatory kit.
  • Cutting the course off the marked itinerary.
  • Receiving outside assistance away from the crew zones.
  • Missing a checkpoint — 2 to 3 hours per checkpoint missed.

Disqualified OUT OF THE RACE

  • Throwing litter outside the assigned points.
  • Using any means of transport for any part of the course.
  • Unsporting behaviour, disrespecting nature, or any discourtesy to organisers, volunteers or other runners.
  • Finishing after the course closes, or passing a control after its closing time.
  • Failing to help or assist an injured runner.
  • Altering the course markings.
  • Losing your bib or timing chip, or running under someone else's bib.

WHAT YOU'RE SIGNING UP FOR

Entry requires accepting, in writing, that you know the length, climb and terrain and are physically and mentally prepared; that you have real mountain experience; that you can cope unaided with altitude, night, wind, cold, fog, rain or snow; and that you can manage your own fatigue, digestive trouble, muscle and joint pain and minor wounds, even alone. The fee is not a support contract: the organisation's role is not to help a participant manage their problems — safety depends on your capacity to adapt to what the mountain presents.
